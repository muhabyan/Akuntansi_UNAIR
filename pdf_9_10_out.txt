🞂 Consumption is an activity that destroyed 
utility of goods and services.
🞂 Personal consumption expenditure is the 
expenditure (pengeluaran) of households to 
purchase non durable and durable goods 
(except new housing) and services.
🞂 A linear consumption function:
C = C0 + bY
where, C0 = autonomous consumption; b = 
marginal propensity to consume (MPC), and 
0< b < 1; Y = disposable income 
Consumption

--- PAGE BREAK ---

🞂 Personal saving is the part of disposable 
income that is not consumed
🞂 Therefore, the saving function can be 
derived as follows:
S = Y – C
S = Y - C0 + bY
S = -C0 + (1– b)Y
where, -C0 = dissaving (or negative saving); 
(1-b) = marginal propensity to save (MPS), 
and 0< b < 1

🞂 Macroeconomist use the term ‘investment’ or ‘real 
investment’ to mean additions to the stock of 
productive assets or capital goods  (=the purchase 
of new capital) such as equipment or buildings.
◦ When Amazon.com builds a new warehouse or 
when the Smiths build a new house, these activities 
represent investment
Investment

--- PAGE BREAK ---

🞂 Many people speak of “investing” when buying a 
piece of land, stock, or using money to open a 
saving account.
◦ In economics, these purchases are really financial 
transactions or “financial investments,” because what one 
person is buying, someone else is selling, and the net effect 
is zero.
◦ There is investment only when real capital is produced

🞂 We must distinguish tangible and intangible 
assets from financial assets.
◦ Financial assets are essentially pieces of paper or 
electronic records.
◦ More precisely, financial assets are monetary claims 
by one party against another party

homeowner for monthly payments of interest and 
principal; these payments will repay the original loan 
that helped finance the purchase of the house.
● In this case, a tangible asset will serve as collateral for 
financial asset.

🞂 The rate of return on investment is the annual 
net dollar return (revenue less expenses) per 
year for every dollar of invested capital.
◦ An example: a rental car company ‘KARTIKA’ buys a 
used car for $20,000 and rent it out. After 
subtracting all expenses (revenue less wages, office 
supplies, energy cost, etc) and assuming no change 
in the car’s price, this company earns a net rental of 
$2400 each year. Thus, the rate of return is
$2,400/$20,000 = 12%
🞂 Interest rate are the rate of return on financial 
assets, measured in percent per year.

🞂 Interest Rates and Asset Prices
◦ Asset prices tend to move inversely with interest rates because their 
present value decrease as the interest rate increases (see examples in 
Samuelson (ch.15 p.287)
◦ The dependence of asset prices on interest rate is a general property 
of financial assets.
◦ The price of stocks, bonds, real estate, and many other long-lived 
assets will decline as interest rates rise.
◦ Harga saham, obligasi, real estat, dan banyak aset berumur panjang 
lainnya akan turun seiring dengan kenaikan suku bunga.
🞂 Real vs. Nominal Interest Rates
🞂 Nominal interest rate (sometime also called the money interest 
rate) is the interest rate on money in terms of money
🞂 Real interest rate is the nominal interest rate after corrected 
for inflation

🞂 Principles of taxation
◦ Benefit vs. Ability to pay principles
● The benefit principle of taxation taxes people in 
proportion to the marginal benefits they receive from a 
governmentally provided good.
● The ability-to-pay principle suggests that the fairest 
tax is one based on your financial ability to support 
government activities.
◦ Horizontal and vertical equity
● Vertical equity asserts that people better able to pay 
higher taxes should do so.
● Horizontal equity requires that individuals who are 
equal in all important respects be treated equally.
Important to note:
(SAM, ch. 16 p. 312-320)

--- PAGE BREAK ---

◦ Pragmatic compromise in taxation
● Progressive 🡪 higher incomes are taxed proportionally 
more than lower incomes
● regressive 🡪 the percentage of income paid as taxes 
falls as income rises.
● proportional taxes 🡪 a fixed percentage of income

Fiscal Policy Multiplier 
Assumption: tax is independence to income (T =T0)
🞂 Government expenditure multiplier
Y = 1/(1-b) (C0 – bT0 + I + G)
Y + ΔY = 1/(1-b) (C0 – bT0 + I + G + ΔG)
ΔY = 1/(1-b) ΔG

multiplier
🞂 Tax multiplier
Y = 1/(1-b) (C0 – bT0 + I + G)

ΔY = -b/(1-b) ΔT0
where: -b/(1-b) is tax multiplier.

🞂 National income identity in the closed economy
Y = C + I + G
🞂 Subtraction C and G form both side of the 
equation, we obtain:
Y – C – G = I
🞂 Manipulating this equation to obtain
(Y – T – C) + (T – G) = I
Where the left hand side is national saving, which consists of 
private saving (Y – T – C) and public saving (T – G) 
Important to note:

--- PAGE BREAK ---

🞂 The last equation reveals an important fact:
◦ For the economy as a whole, saving must be equal 
to investment. (the mechanisms lie behind this 
identity will be discussed in the next topic)
◦ The larger the consumption, the smaller private 
saving, and the result would be lower national 
saving
◦ when the government spends more than it receives 
in tax revenue, the resulting budget deficit lower 
national saving
